Justin Robinson

Welcome to my website!

I'm a postdoctoral researcher at Nuffield College, University of Oxford, working on the Economic Insecurity: Finding Answers and Solutions project at the Nuffield Politics Research Centre.

My work sits within the fields of political behaviour and political psychology, and coalesces around three main themes:

  • Economic voting, political economy and the way that economic insecurity shapes political preferences, with a focus on Britain.
  • Psychological explanations of why people support the parties and policies that they do, with a particular focus on upbringing and socialization.
  • Understanding how the intersection of individual-level experiences and wider social or political context shapes political behaviour.

Methodologically, I'm interested in advanced statistical approaches with a focus on causal inference using large-N observational data.

Having completed my PhD at the University of York in early 2025, I have so far published in the British Journal of Political Science, Political Behavior, Electoral Studies and Ethnic and Racial Studies.

I'm also interested in generating impact beyond the academy. In addition to an ongoing collaboration with the Joseph Rowntree Foundation, my research has been featured in The Conversation, The Observer, and The New Statesman.

I have experience teaching quantitative methods (R and Stata), comparative politics and political behaviour.
